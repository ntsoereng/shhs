Our History

Sacred Heart High School is a Roman Catholic Institution founded by the Reverend Fr. André Coindre, a French diocesan priest in Lyon, France, in 1821.

We are dedicated to the Christian education of young men who are morally upright, academically sound, socially balanced, and physically capable. The school is under the auspices of the Leribe Diocese Bishop and is conducted by the Brothers of the Sacred Heart in partnership with lay collaborators.

Following the ideology of Brother Henry SC, also considered the "father" and founder of the school who laid the foundation and the rhythm of the school, the school emphasises a system of free discipline. We strive that, as a team, we work around the clock to systematically and progressively better our results. Our young men are prepared to respect, account for, and be transparent in their dealings with minimum supervision. We don't believe in rigid supervision of students as that can be detrimental to their character development.

We therefore adopted a liberal tradition at the very time of inception, and 192 years later, the tradition has been maintained. We are among the most prestigious schools in Lesotho, owing to our excellent academic performance and dominance in sporting disciplines. Many of the alumni who have passed through Sacred Heart High School went on to occupy crucial civil service, society and political positions.
